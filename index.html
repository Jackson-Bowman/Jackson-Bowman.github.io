Star Trek Timelines

For the Summer of 2018 I was an Engineering Intern at Disruptor Beam Inc. working on Star Trek Timelines. Included below is a sample of my contributions to the project.

Double Rewards

This feature allows players to double the rewards they gained from a mission by watching a short advertisement. Some of the challenges of writing this feature involved writing tight back-end code to track the most recent mission rewards as well as protect against potential exploitation of API endpoints by malicious users.

Achievements

Previous Next

I added two achievements to Star Trek Timelines during my internship. The first was an achievement for changing the name of the Captain (player character). The idea behind it is to encourage players to change their name from the default to something more unique and identifiable, making any future interactions with the support team a bit easier. The other achievement was created to reward high-commitment players who had maxed out their reputation with the game's various factions. Both of these achievments also involved writing scripts to award the achievements to players who had already reached the respective milestones prior to their addition to the game client.

Event Pack Tool

This tool was developed using React to improve the content pipeline for new event packs. It was made in close cooperation with the design team. Some of the requirements involved supporting multiple output formats, robust error checking, and some small quality of life features to ensure an efficient experience for the tool's users. The Event Pack creator was also part of a larger react project which added additional modularity and extensibility requirements.

Other Projects


Mazu

Mazu is a top-down action-adventure game inspired by the bullet hell genre and Asian mythology. As part of OxHead studios, I developed the player's movement and abilities, working alongside the designers to ensure a smooth playtesting process. Additionally I organized our version control workflow and maintain our build pipeline on Itch.io. Mazu was developed in unity with scripting in C#. You can learn more about Mazu on our official website

Return

Created during Global Game Jam 2018, Return is a game where you “transmit” yourself into forest animals in order to overcome environmental obstacles. The game was developed in Unity with 3-D models created in Blender. I worked on scripting (C#) and level design. The game is playable in browser on itch.io.

ICEELand

ICEELand is a platformer where the goal is to avoid enemies to survive as long as possible. The game was made using SDL and C++ with art assets from Glitch . I designed the level, led much of the design of the game's architecture and programmed the player, environment,and rendering elements. You can find the final release here.

Scribe

Scribe is a demo of a gesture recognition system where you enchant crystals by drawing symbols on the screen. The game was made in the Unity Engine using the PDollar package provided by Da Viking Code.  You can download the game from my itch.io page.

Beautiful Day

Beautiful day is a short narrative game I made to test the macro capabilities of Twine. The game is playable in browser on itch.io.

Race to Destruction

Race to Destruction is a board game I worked on with a team of classmates. Our main design goal was to make a game that was playable in around 30 min while still maintaining strategic depth and allowing for moments of tension and suspense. The basic premise is that each player controls a faction which is competing to collect three components to a super-weapon that will allow them to eliminate their rivals. Components spawn at random positions on the board and players have a number of methods at their disposal to impede the progress of other players. My team was composed of four individuals with me taking on the role of lead designer. You can find a link to the rulebook  here.

Extreme Concentration

Extreme Concentration is based on the well-known match-2 card game This version seeks to make the game significantly more difficult by requiring the player to solve the whole board at once without any mistakes. To balance the difficulty, players have a short period where they can view every card and attempt to memorize their locations. Once the timer on the period runs out, the cards will flip back face-down and two cards will switch places with each other at random. I developed Extreme Concentration in Game Maker Studio and made all the art assets in Paint.NET. You can download the game here.

Sanctify

Sanctify is a spectral tabletop rpg about phantoms, curses, and powerful decision making. One of my biggest focuses in designing this rpg was to afford a unique campaign structure based around the game's setting. Campaigns in sanctify revolve around the appearance of phantoms, souls of the dead who have returned to the world of the living, and the curses that bring them there. The player characters are priests tasked with dispelling curses and solving the dilemmas caused by the appearance of phantoms. 

 Sanctify is heavily inspired by Dungeon World and is backwards compatible with most monsters, items, and player classes from that system.  While I definitely think Sanctify needs more work I have the beginnings of an srd and the first iteration of the character sheet completed. All design on Sanctify was done by me.
